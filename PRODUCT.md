# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone reading academic papers: researchers scanning new publications, ML practitioners keeping up with arxiv, students learning to parse dense literature. They arrive with a PDF or link, want structured extraction fast, and spend most of their time reading the output. Sessions can be long. Eye comfort matters more than visual flash.

## Product Purpose

A local-first research paper analyzer that extracts structured, evidence-backed summaries using Ollama. It exists so readers can quickly decide whether a paper deserves a deep read, understand its contributions without wading through jargon, and reference key findings later. Success: the user reads the output comfortably and trusts the extraction.

## Brand Personality

Readable, quiet, human. A calm reading surface that respects the content. Not a SaaS dashboard, not a terminal, not a marketing page. The interface disappears; the extracted knowledge stays.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI/SaaS tools: the blue-600 + slate-gray + rounded-card template that every Cursor/v0-generated app ships with
- Terminal / hacker aesthetic: monospace everything, dark backgrounds, code-editor vibes
- Over-designed marketing: gradient text, glassmorphism, animated distractions, style over substance
- AI-slop typography: the geometric sans-serif + tracked uppercase + muted gray body text that signals "generated, not designed"

## Design Principles

1. **Reading comfort first** — every decision serves long-form readability: type size, line length, contrast, spacing
2. **Content is the interface** — the extracted paper analysis IS the product; chrome exists only to support it
3. **Quiet confidence** — the tool works locally, privately, without fanfare; the design reflects that restraint
4. **Honest hierarchy** — sections have clear weight differences so scanning is effortless; no flat sameness
5. **Human warmth without decoration** — approachable through typography and rhythm, not through ornament or illustration

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text and UI components)
- Comfortable for extended reading sessions: no harsh contrast extremes, no pure white backgrounds
- Reduced motion support for all animations
- Works well at 200% zoom and on narrow viewports
